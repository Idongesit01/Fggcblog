2002 Set celebrates 20th Anniversary

The year 2022 marked a significant milestone for the old girls' set of 2002, as they celebrated their 20th anniversary since graduating from school. The event was a grand reunion that brought together former classmates to relive cherished memories and express gratitude for the journey so far. The all-white party provided an opportunity for the girls to reconnect and draw inspiration from speakers, while the thanksgiving service was a solemn moment to express gratitude to God for the journey so far. The donation of classroom lockers was a generous gesture that demonstrated the girls' commitment to giving back to their alma mater. The event was a testament to the strong bonds and enduring friendships that have kept the old girls' set of 2002 united over the past two decades.

The All-White Party

On the 6th of August 2022, the old girls' set of 2002 gathered for an "all-white" party, which was a major highlight of the anniversary celebration. The event featured talk-sessions from renowned speakers who inspired the girls to aim for greater heights in their personal and professional lives. The speakers shared personal experiences and success stories, emphasizing the importance of hard work, determination, and perseverance. The talks were followed by a fun-filled night of dancing, networking, and reconnecting with old friends.

The Thanksgiving Service

On the 7th of August 2022, the old girls' set of 2002 attended a thanksgiving service at St Anthony Catholic Church, Ewet Housing Estate, Uyo. The service was a solemn moment to express gratitude to God for sustaining the girls over the past twenty years. The girls offered thanksgiving prayers, sang hymns, and listened to a sermon that emphasized the importance of gratitude and living a purposeful life. The service was a reminder of the role of faith in the girls' lives, and they left feeling rejuvenated and spiritually uplifted.

The Donation of Classroom Lockers

As part of the commemoration project, the old girls' set of 2002 donated fifty (50) sets of classroom lockers to the school. The donation was a show of gratitude to their alma mater for the foundational education that has shaped their lives. The lockers were received by the school's principal, Engr Mrs. Opute, who expressed her gratitude to the old girls' set of 2002 for their generous donation. The lockers will go a long way in providing students with a secure place to store their personal belongings during class hours.
